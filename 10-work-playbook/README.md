# 10 — Behavior Frontiers Playbook (starts week one on the job)

This module is P1 — the AI Adoption Program — run deliberately enough that
its paper trail becomes your **AI & Organizational Transformation
Presentation** and **Managing AI Engineering Project**, nearly for free.
The work itself is document-shaped; the documents are the deliverables.

The meta-move: most people do this job reactively and have nothing to show
after a year but vibes. You run it like a program, and every quarter
produces artifacts. Same hours, compounding output.

## The operating loop (repeat per department)

**1. Discover** — structured pain-point interviews (template:
`discovery-interview-template.md`). 30–45 min per person, 2–4 people per
department. You're hunting for: repetitive text work, lookup drudgery,
handoff friction, "I keep a personal cheat sheet because the official docs
are unusable" (← that one is Companion's market research).

**2. Prioritize** — score candidate workflows on the 2×2: impact × feasibility,
with a hard modifier: **anything touching client PHI drops a feasibility
tier** (higher bar, slower path, more approvals — not never, just not first).
First pilots should be PHI-free: scheduling comms, internal docs, onboarding,
report *formatting* (not content).

**3. Pilot** — one workflow, one department, 2–4 weeks, defined before you
start via `pilot-one-pager.md`: baseline metric, target, owner, kill
criteria. Small enough to fail safely; instrumented enough that success is a
number, not an anecdote.

**4. Train** — a session per rollout: live demo on THEIR real task, hands-on
exercise, a one-page cheat sheet, and a named "local champion" per
department. Collect the questions people ask — they're simultaneously your
training-gap map and Companion-Work's eval dataset.

**5. Govern** — the boring thing that makes you promotable: an approved-use
register (what's deployed where, what data it touches, who owns it), a
lightweight review step for anything new, an incident path. In a HIPAA org
this isn't optional, and being the person who *built* the register beats
being the person who got asked where it is.

## Cadence

- Weekly: 30 min Friday — update the register, log one insight in work notes.
- Monthly: one-page "AI program update" to the CTO (shipped, measured,
  next, risks). Twelve of these ≈ the Org Transformation presentation,
  pre-written.
- Quarterly: re-run the prioritization with fresh discovery data.

## Week-one checklist (do these before building anything)

- [ ] CTO conversation: the firewall agreement (what can be generalized for
      Quantic coursework, in writing) + where the approved-use register lives
- [ ] Get the org chart; book discovery interviews with 2 departments
- [ ] Find out what already exists: shadow AI usage (people are already
      pasting things into chatbots — you want amnesty-style honesty, not a
      crackdown), existing vendor tools, any past AI attempts and why they died
- [ ] Ask engineering: deployment constraints, data boundaries, who reviews code

## Quantic mapping (be opportunistic, not premature)

When each unit opens, pull from the trail: *Adopting AI in Your Organization*
→ discovery + prioritization docs. *User-Centered Design* → training-session
observation notes. *Professionalism & Ethics* → the PHI modifier + register.
*AI Leadership and Management* → the champion network + monthly updates.
*Org Transformation Presentation* → the whole arc, quarter by quarter.
Scrub per the firewall; get the sign-off; submit work you already did.
